Biological Clock Testing

Determine Your True Biological Age

Your Biological vs Chronological Age

Your chronological age is simply how many years you've lived. Your biological age, however, reflects how quickly your cells are aging at the molecular level. Two people of identical chronological age can have vastly different biological ages—one with youthful cells, another with aged, dysfunctional cells.

Our advanced biological clock testing uses cutting-edge epigenetic and molecular analysis to determine your true biological age. This reveals whether you're aging faster or slower than your years, identifies accelerated aging factors, and tracks how interventions reverse aging at the cellular level.

What We Measure

Epigenetic Age

Analyzes DNA methylation patterns to determine cellular aging rate independent of genetics.

Telomere Length

Measures chromosome caps reflecting how many divisions cells have undergone and aging potential.

Mitochondrial Health

Assesses energy production capacity and cellular vitality—crucial for longevity.

Inflammatory Markers

Measures systemic inflammation driving aging—key predictor of disease and mortality.

Senescence Index

Quantifies accumulated senescent cells—zombie cells causing tissue dysfunction and aging.

NAD+ & Energy Status

Measures cellular energy currency indicating metabolic health and longevity potential.

Understanding Biological Age Markers

1

Epigenetic Clocks

DNA methylation-based aging clocks (Horvath, Hannum, PhenoAge) measure the pace of biological aging with exceptional accuracy, predicting lifespan better than chronological age.

2

Telomere Assessment

Telomere length in white blood cells indicates cellular division capacity—shorter telomeres associated with aging and disease. Telomere length can be extended through lifestyle intervention.

3

Inflammatory Status

Chronic low-grade inflammation ("inflammaging") accelerates aging. Measuring inflammatory markers guides anti-inflammatory intervention strategies.

4

Metabolic Age

Assessment of mitochondrial function, metabolic rate, and glucose control—fundamental to aging pace. Poor metabolic health accelerates biological aging.

5

Cellular Senescence

Senescent cells accumulate with age, causing tissue dysfunction and disease. Measuring senescence markers guides senolytic therapies.

6

Longevity Phenotype

Integration of all markers creates comprehensive biological age profile predicting healthspan and lifespan potential.

Aging is Not Inevitable

Biological clock testing reveals that aging pace is modifiable. Many people reverse their biological age by 5-10 years through lifestyle changes. This test motivates intervention by showing tangible proof that you're getting younger at the cellular level.

Age Reversal Strategies

Exercise & Movement

High-intensity interval training, strength training, and daily movement dramatically improve biological age markers.

Nutrition Optimization

Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant-rich diet reducing processed foods and excess sugar.

Sleep Enhancement

7-9 hours quality sleep dramatically improves epigenetic markers and longevity outcomes.

Stress Management

Chronic stress accelerates aging. Meditation, mindfulness, and relaxation reverse stress aging.

Targeted Supplementation

NAD+ boosters, senolytics, antioxidants, and longevity compounds targeting aging pathways.

Continuous Monitoring

Repeat biological clock testing every 6-12 months tracks aging reversal and intervention effectiveness.

FAQ

Can biological age actually be reversed?
Yes! Multiple studies show that intensive lifestyle interventions can reverse biological age by 3-10 years. Targeted supplementation and interventions can provide additional improvements.
How long until I see results?
Initial improvements in biomarkers occur within 2-4 weeks. Significant biological age reversal typically takes 3-6 months of consistent intervention.
How often should I retest?
Initial baseline testing, then follow-up every 6-12 months to track aging reversal and adjust strategies based on response.
Does this predict my lifespan?
Biological age is highly predictive of lifespan and healthspan. However, it's not deterministic—it shows your current aging trajectory, which you can change through intervention.
