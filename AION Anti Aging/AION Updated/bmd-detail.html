Bone Mineral Density Testing

Assess Bone Strength & Osteoporosis Risk

Bone Health & Longevity

Your bones aren't just structural—they're living tissue actively involved in mineral metabolism, immune function, and longevity. Bone loss accelerates with age, especially post-menopause, increasing fracture risk, disability, and mortality. Yet bone density is highly modifiable with proper intervention.

Our BMD testing measures bone mineral density in critical locations (hip, spine, forearm) using DEXA scanning, identifies your osteoporosis risk, and guides targeted interventions to preserve or rebuild bone strength throughout life.

Key Measurements

Spine BMD

Measures lumbar spine bone density, critical for posture and spinal stability.

Hip BMD

Assesses femoral neck and hip bone density, most predictive of fracture risk.

Forearm BMD

Measures wrist bone density for additional fracture risk assessment.

T-Score & Z-Score

Compares your bone density to healthy young adults and age-matched populations.

Fracture Risk Assessment

FRAX scores predict 10-year fracture probability guiding intervention urgency.

Density Tracking

Monitor how interventions affect bone density over time with follow-up scans.

Understanding BMD Results

1

Normal Bone Density

T-score of -1.0 or higher. Bones are healthy and fracture risk is low with current lifestyle.

2

Osteopenia (Bone Loss)

T-score between -1.0 and -2.5. Early bone loss detected—time for intensive preventive measures.

3

Osteoporosis

T-score below -2.5. Significant bone loss with increased fracture risk requiring medical intervention.

4

Severe Osteoporosis

T-score below -3.0 with history of fractures. Aggressive treatment and prevention essential.

Bone Loss is Preventable

Even with osteoporosis diagnosis, bone density can be improved through resistance training, adequate calcium/vitamin D, hormonal optimization, and targeted supplementation. Early intervention prevents fractures and maintains independence.

Bone Building Strategies

Resistance Training

Mechanical stress through weight-bearing and strength exercises stimulates bone formation.

Nutrient Optimization

Calcium, vitamin D, magnesium, K2, and other minerals essential for bone formation.

Hormonal Balance

Estrogen, testosterone, and thyroid hormone optimization crucial for bone maintenance.

Inflammation Reduction

Chronic inflammation accelerates bone loss—anti-inflammatory diet and interventions essential.

The Testing Process

Scan Duration

DEXA scan takes approximately 10-15 minutes, completely painless and non-invasive.

Radiation Exposure

DEXA uses minimal radiation—about 1/10th of a chest X-ray. Safe for regular monitoring.

Comprehensive Report

Detailed results showing bone density, T-scores, Z-scores, and fracture risk assessment.

Personalized Plan

Consultation reviewing results and creating targeted bone health optimization strategy.

FAQ

Who should get BMD testing?
Women over 65 and men over 70 should have screening. Earlier testing recommended if you have risk factors (early menopause, family history, low body weight, steroid use).
How often should I be tested?
Initial baseline testing, then repeat every 1-2 years if normal, or more frequently if osteopenia/osteoporosis detected to track intervention response.
Can I improve my bone density?
Yes! Resistance training, adequate nutrition, hormone balance, and lifestyle modifications can halt bone loss and improve density by 5-10% within 1-2 years.
Is medication always necessary?
Not always. Early-stage bone loss can often be reversed with aggressive lifestyle interventions. Medication is considered based on severity and fracture risk.
