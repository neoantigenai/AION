Body Composition Analysis

Measure Muscle, Fat, and Metabolic Health

Body Composition & Aging

Your body weight tells only part of the story. Two people at identical weight can have vastly different body compositions—one lean and muscular, one overfat with poor metabolic health. Body Composition Analysis (BCA) measures muscle mass, fat mass, water percentage, and bone density to reveal your true health status.

Muscle loss (sarcopenia) is one of the most significant markers of aging, increasing fall risk, frailty, and mortality. Excess fat, especially visceral fat, drives inflammation and disease. Our BCA precisely measures these parameters and tracks how interventions affect your composition.

What We Measure

Lean Muscle Mass

Quantifies muscle tissue needed for strength, metabolism, and functional independence.

Body Fat Percentage

Measures total and visceral fat indicating disease risk beyond weight alone.

Total Body Water

Assesses cellular hydration indicating cellular health and metabolic function.

Bone Density

Measures bone mineral content important for strength and osteoporosis prevention.

Metabolic Rate

Calculates your basal metabolic rate based on body composition for personalized nutrition.

Composition Tracking

Monitor how diet and exercise affect muscle gain versus fat loss over time.

Advanced Analysis Methods

1

DEXA Scanning

Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DEXA) provides precise measurement of bone, muscle, and fat mass with exceptional accuracy and low radiation exposure.

2

InBody Analysis

Bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) quickly measures body water distribution and cellular health based on electrical properties of body tissues.

3

Visceral Fat Assessment

Special attention to dangerous visceral fat surrounding organs—strongly linked to metabolic syndrome and cardiovascular disease.

4

Segmental Analysis

Measurement by body segment (arms, legs, trunk) identifies specific areas of muscle loss or fat accumulation requiring targeted intervention.

The Muscle-Longevity Connection

Maintaining muscle mass is one of the strongest predictors of longevity and quality of life in aging. Resistance training combined with adequate protein intake directly combats sarcopenia and metabolic decline.

Optimization Strategies

Resistance Training

Targeted strength training protocols to build muscle and improve metabolic rate.

Protein Optimization

Adequate protein intake to support muscle synthesis and preserve lean mass.

Fat Loss Strategies

Evidence-based approaches to reduce fat while preserving muscle tissue.

Metabolic Enhancement

Supplementation and lifestyle changes to optimize metabolic rate and energy production.

The Testing Process

Scan Duration

DEXA and InBody scans take only 10-15 minutes, completely painless and non-invasive.

Preparation

Wear comfortable clothing, remove metal objects. No fasting required but should be hydrated.

Detailed Report

Comprehensive printout showing muscle, fat, bone density, and metabolic calculations.

Progress Tracking

Repeat scans every 3-6 months to track composition changes from your training program.

FAQ

Is the DEXA scan safe?
Yes, DEXA uses very low radiation exposure—about 1/10th of a chest X-ray. The scan is completely safe and painless.
How often should I retest?
Initial scan establishes baseline. Repeat testing every 3-6 months tracks progress from exercise and nutrition interventions.
What's a healthy body fat percentage?
Generally, 18-25% for women and 10-20% for men, though optimal varies by age and fitness level. We'll guide you to your target.
Can I improve my results?
Absolutely! Resistance training, adequate protein, and caloric balance can significantly improve body composition within weeks.
