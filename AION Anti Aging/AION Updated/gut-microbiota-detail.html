Gut Microbiota Analysis

Understanding Your Microbiome Composition

The Microbiome & Longevity

Your gut microbiota—the trillions of bacteria living in your digestive tract—is far more than just digestive aides. These microorganisms influence your immune system, mental health, metabolism, inflammation levels, and aging speed. Recent research has identified microbiome composition as one of the strongest predictors of healthy aging.

An imbalanced microbiome (dysbiosis) is linked to obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cognitive decline, and premature aging. Our comprehensive microbiota analysis identifies bacterial imbalances and provides targeted interventions to restore healthy microbial diversity.

What We Measure

Bacterial Diversity

Higher microbial diversity strongly correlates with better health outcomes and longevity.

Species Composition

Identification of specific bacterial phyla and their proportions for optimal gut ecology.

Dysbiosis Markers

Detection of harmful pathogenic bacteria or beneficial bacteria deficiencies.

Functional Metabolites

Analysis of beneficial compounds produced by microbiota like short-chain fatty acids.

Immune Markers

Assessment of gut barrier integrity and immune-microbiota interactions.

Inflammation Indicators

Markers indicating systemic inflammation stemming from gut dysbiosis.

The Testing Process

1

Stool Sample Collection

A convenient at-home stool sample is collected using our provided kit and mailed to our laboratory.

2

DNA Sequencing Analysis

Advanced 16S rRNA sequencing identifies and quantifies bacterial species present in your microbiota.

3

Comprehensive Report

Results include microbiota composition, diversity scores, comparison to healthy reference populations, and dysbiosis indicators.

4

Personalized Recommendations

Our specialists provide targeted dietary changes, probiotic recommendations, and lifestyle modifications to restore balance.

The Gut-Brain-Aging Axis

Your gut microbiota communicates with your brain through the vagus nerve, influencing mood, cognitive function, and neuroinflammation—all critical factors in brain aging. A healthy microbiome is essential for optimal neurological longevity.

Restoration Strategies

Dietary Interventions

Prebiotic foods, fermented foods, and fiber-rich vegetables feed beneficial bacteria and restore diversity.

Probiotic Supplementation

Targeted probiotic strains selected based on your specific dysbiosis patterns.

Lifestyle Modifications

Stress reduction, sleep optimization, and exercise—all powerfully influence microbiota composition.

Microbiota Monitoring

Follow-up testing after 3-6 months of interventions to track restoration progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I test my microbiota?
Initial testing establishes baseline. Follow-up testing after 3-6 months of interventions tracks progress. Annual testing is recommended for ongoing monitoring.
Can probiotics restore my microbiota?
Probiotics are one component of microbiota restoration. Diet changes, lifestyle modifications, and sometimes antimicrobial treatments are equally important for lasting restoration.
Is the sample collection uncomfortable?
No, collection is done at home using a convenient kit. Detailed instructions ensure proper sampling and minimal inconvenience.
How long until results?
Laboratory analysis takes 2-3 weeks. A comprehensive report and consultation follow within 5-7 business days.
