Genetic Test

Comprehensive Genetic Profiling for Personalized Wellness

Understanding Your Genetic Blueprint

Your genes contain the instruction manual for how your body functions, ages, and responds to environmental challenges. Our comprehensive genetic testing analyzes key markers that influence longevity, metabolism, nutrient absorption, stress response, and susceptibility to age-related diseases.

This isn't about predicting your future—it's about understanding your genetic potential and optimizing your lifestyle, nutrition, and supplementation to work with your unique genetic profile. By identifying genetic variations (SNPs), we can personalize every aspect of your anti-aging strategy.

What Our Genetic Testing Reveals

Metabolism Optimization

Discover your genetic metabolic rate, how you process different macronutrients, and your optimal diet composition.

Nutrient Processing

Identify genetic variations affecting nutrient absorption and methylation, guiding supplementation strategies.

Cardiovascular Health

Understand genetic predispositions to cholesterol metabolism and heart disease risk factors.

Cognitive Health

Genetic markers related to cognitive decline risk and neuroinflammation help guide neuroprotective strategies.

Sleep & Circadian Rhythm

Discover your chronotype and genetic sleep patterns for optimized rest and recovery protocols.

Weight Management

Genetic factors influencing satiety, appetite regulation, and metabolic efficiency guide personalized weight strategies.

Key Genetic Markers Analyzed

1

Methylation & Detoxification

MTHFR, COMT, MAO genes that impact your ability to process methylation, handle stress neurotransmitters, and eliminate toxins efficiently.

2

Inflammation Markers

IL-6, TNF-alpha, and other inflammatory cytokine genes that influence aging speed and chronic disease risk.

3

Cardiovascular Risk

APOE, LDLR, and lipid metabolism genes indicating genetic predisposition to cholesterol management challenges.

4

Insulin & Glucose Control

Genetic variations affecting insulin sensitivity, glucose metabolism, and diabetes risk help guide nutritional strategy.

5

Antioxidant Defense

SOD, CAT, GPX genes determining your genetic antioxidant capacity and free radical defense efficiency.

6

Bone Health

VDR, COL1A1 genes influencing bone density and osteoporosis risk, especially important for aging populations.

Gene Expression vs. Genetic Code

Remember: having a genetic variant doesn't determine your fate. Epigenetics shows us that lifestyle, nutrition, stress management, and exercise can activate or silence genes. Our genetic testing is the beginning of an optimization journey, not a sentence.

The Testing Process

Sample Collection

A simple saliva sample or buccal swab (cheek swab) is collected—no blood draw necessary. The sample is then sent to our certified laboratory for analysis.

Laboratory Analysis

Your DNA is analyzed for 100+ relevant genetic markers using cutting-edge sequencing technology. Results typically available within 2-3 weeks.

Interpretation Report

We provide a comprehensive, easy-to-understand report explaining your genetic variants and what they mean for your health and longevity.

Consultation

Our genetic specialists conduct an in-depth consultation to explain results and create a personalized action plan for optimizing your genetic potential.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is genetic testing safe and private?
Yes. Your genetic information is protected under strict privacy regulations. We use industry-leading encryption and follow HIPAA compliance standards. Your genetic data is never sold or shared without explicit consent.
Will this testing be covered by insurance?
Some comprehensive genetic tests may be covered if medically indicated. We can discuss insurance possibilities during your consultation and provide documentation if needed.
Can my genes be changed?
No, your genetic code cannot be changed through conventional means. However, epigenetics shows that lifestyle factors can activate or suppress gene expression, which is what we optimize through our personalized protocols.
Should I test my family members?
Genetic testing can be valuable for family members, as some conditions run in families. We can discuss which family members might benefit from testing based on your results.
How often should I retest?
Your genetic code doesn't change, so retesting isn't needed. However, your interpretation and optimization strategies should be updated as new genetic research emerges and as your health goals evolve.
